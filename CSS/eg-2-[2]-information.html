Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was a pioneering 17th-century Indian warrior-king and the founder of the Maratha
Empire, hailed for establishing Hindavi Swarajya (self-rule) amid the dominant Mughal and Deccan Sultanate rule. Born on February
19, 1630, at the Shivneri Fort in Pune, he was raised by his mother, Jijabai, who imbued him with values of justice, bravery,
and religious devotion. Displaying early leadership, Shivaji began capturing strategic forts like Torna, Kondana, and Purandar by
the age of 16, utilizing a small, loyal force from the Maval region. Vedantu Vedantu +4 His military brilliance was defined by his pioneering use of guerrilla tactics, known as Ganimi Kava, which enabled his smaller army to surprise

and outmaneuver massive, well-equipped armies, including those of the Sultanate of Bijapur and the Mughal Empire. A defining moment in his
early career was the 1659 Battle of Pratapgarh, where he defeated and killed Afzal Khan, a veteran general sent by the Bijapur Sultanate
to capture him. This victory gained him immense reputation and resources, solidifying his foothold in the region. Wikipedia Wikipedia +4 Shivaji's conflict with the powerful Mughal Empire under Aurangzeb was marked by daring exploits, most notably a daring 1663 night raid on
Shaista Khan in Pune, where the Mughal governor lost his fingers, and the audacious 1666 escape from house arrest in Agra with his son, Sambhaji.
After the Treaty of Purandar (1665), where he was temporarily forced to cede forts, he reclaimed them and expanded his dominion, including a
significant second sacking of Surat in 1670. Wikipedia Wikipedia +1 Formally crowned as the Chhatrapati (Sovereign Monarch) on June 6, 1674, at Raigad Fort, he established a stable and progressive state with an
Ashtapradhan (Council of Eight Ministers) to assist in governance. As a ruler, he was highly forward-thinking, establishing a strong navy to
protect the Konkan coastline from sea-based threats and foreign colonial powers, earning him the title 'Father of the Indian Navy'. in key army and navy roles, and respected religious freedom while strictly enforcing ethical governance. Shivaji was also recognized for his
chivalrous treatment of women and strict enforcement against violence towards them in captured territories. He passed away on April 3, 1680,
at Raigad Fort, leaving behind a resilient kingdom that continued to challenge Mughal dominance for decades.
